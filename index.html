Unemployment

Occurs when a person who is currently unemployed is unable to find work. Those who are considered unemployed are people who have been actively searching for work in the past four weeks, are available to work and people who were temporarily laid off. Unemployment is used as a measure of the health of the economy. High rates of unemployment can signal economic distress while extremely low levels can also signal an overheated economy.

Types of Unemployed Workers

  • Job Loser
  • Job Leaver
  • Reentrant
  • New Entrant


Types of Unemployment

  • Structural Unemployment: Occurs in the economy when there is a mismatch between skills of the unemployed and job openings. A long recession can often create structural unemployment.
  • Frictional Unemployment: is caused by delays in matching available jobs and workers. Natural part of job-search process and is short-term.
  • Cyclical Unemployment: is caused by economic downturns and is not part of the natural unemployment rate. Cyclical unemployment may tend to create more unemployment.

Unemployment Today 263,000

The unemployment rate fell in April to a 3.6 percent the lowest its been since 1969. The Labor Department reported that the U.S economy had added 263,000 jobs in April, well above what was forcasted by analysts.Hispanic unemployment has dropped to a 4.2 percent, a record low since the 1970s.



Businesses

Thanks to low unemployment, employers are being forced to raise pay and become more aggresive about training and hiring workers. Businesses are finding it increasingly challenging filling up job openings and therefore some have turned to investing in training their own workers. Instead of trying to poach talent from other companies employers are now looking to grow their talent internally.



Not Everbody 3.6

The unemployment rate may be at a 3.6 percent but not for everybody. The African American unemployment rate is at a 6.7 percent making it more than double the rate for whites.

There is still a concern regarding people working multiple jobs to make ends meet. Citing the Labor Department, there are still more than 5 million Americans working more than one job, a number that hasnt changed in recent years.
